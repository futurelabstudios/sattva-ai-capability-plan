Enterprise AI capability development plan

Strategic plan for AI capability development across Sattva.

This document outlines a structured training plan to build practical AI capability across Sattva. The proposed model establishes a common baseline for the wider consulting and business population, then extends into targeted function-specific tracks for finance, marketing, and HR. The objective is to improve productivity, strengthen quality of first-pass work, and create a more consistent operating approach to AI across the organization.

Strategic intent

Build AI capability as an operating capability rather than a one-time awareness exercise.

Sattva's teams work in environments that depend on synthesis, judgment, research, drafting, review, and structured communication. The strategic value of AI in this context lies in improving the speed and consistency of those activities while maintaining appropriate governance, confidentiality, and managerial oversight.

Program architecture

Phased delivery model for scaled training and function-level application.

The plan should be executed as a phased capability program rather than a single event. The core curriculum provides organizational consistency, and the functional tracks translate that baseline into business-relevant workflows.

Phase 1

Leadership alignment and use-case intake

Confirm the population mix, identify representative workflows, and agree the operating guardrails for the program.

Phase 2

AI Essentials rollout

Deliver the 6-hour core curriculum in cohorts across the wider consulting and business population, with selected participation from smaller functions where appropriate.

Phase 3

Consulting and business application track

Run a focused 3-hour track aligned to research, synthesis, proposal development, structured communication, and other recurring delivery workflows.

Phase 4

Specialist function tracks

Deliver targeted 3-hour application tracks for finance, marketing, and HR using use cases specific to each function.

Core curriculum

AI Essentials 6-hour enterprise foundation

The AI Essentials curriculum is intended to establish a common operating baseline across the organization. It should be delivered in repeatable cohorts so the wider consulting and business population can be trained at scale.

The module is designed to cover both capability building and governance: what AI should be used for, how prompts should be structured, how outputs should be reviewed, and where managerial judgment must remain central.

Repeatable cohort model Practical application Governance oriented
  • Common terminology and operating assumptions for AI usage across teams.
  • Standard prompting methods for summarization, drafting, synthesis, and preparation.
  • Shared guidance on verification, confidentiality, and review responsibilities.
  • Clearer identification of high-value use cases for subsequent function-level tracks.

Indicative curriculum coverage

  1. AI fundamentals in a business context: current capability, common limitations, and where AI adds value in knowledge work.
  2. Prompting and instruction design: methods for structuring tasks, context, inputs, and desired outputs.
  3. Workflow integration: practical use of AI in drafting, research support, meeting preparation, and structured analysis.
  4. Governance and review: verification practices, confidentiality discipline, and escalation rules for sensitive work.
  5. Application exercises: guided examples aligned to Sattva's operating environment.

Delivery model

Structured rollout with governance, cohort planning, and use-case alignment.

The training plan should be managed as an enterprise capability initiative. The sequencing, participant mix, and function-level tailoring should be defined in advance to ensure both scale and relevance.

01

Sponsorship and alignment

Confirm sponsorship, target population, and the role of managers in reinforcing adoption after the training sessions.

02

Cohort planning

Schedule repeatable AI Essentials cohorts for the consulting and business population, with smaller specialist sessions for support functions.

03

Use-case based tailoring

Shape each functional track using real workflows and representative examples from the target teams.

04

Governance and measurement

Define review expectations, confidentiality parameters, and a small set of business measures to assess adoption and value.

Execution requirements

Inputs required from Sattva

  • Nomination of representative participants or team leads for each specialist track
  • Examples of representative workflows for consulting and business, finance, marketing, and HR
  • Clear guidance on confidentiality, data sensitivity, and internal review expectations

Expected outcomes

Business outcomes targeted by the plan

  • Consistent AI baseline across the wider consulting and business employee population
  • Higher productivity in drafting, synthesis, preparation, and recurring internal workflows
  • Specialist function-level adoption models for finance, marketing, and HR
  • Clearer governance and review discipline for practical use of AI in day-to-day work
